# GCSE Biology (Combined)  
## Chapter 2: Organisation

---

## 1. The Big Idea (30 seconds)

- Cells are organised into tissues, organs and organ systems  
- Each level works together to keep the organism alive  
- In animals and plants, structures are adapted to their function  

---

## 2. Levels of Organisation

In multicellular organisms:

- **Cells** → basic unit of life  
- **Tissues** → group of similar cells working together  
- **Organs** → different tissues working together  
- **Organ systems** → organs working together  
- **Organism** → the whole living thing  

Example:

- Stomach (organ) is part of the digestive system  

---

## 3. Animal Tissues

Examples of tissues:

- **Muscle tissue**  
  - Contracts to cause movement  

- **Glandular tissue**  
  - Produces substances (e.g. enzymes, hormones)  

- **Epithelial tissue**  
  - Covers surfaces and lines organs  

---

## 4. Organs and Organ Systems

**Digestive system:**

- Breaks down food into small molecules  

Main organs:

- Mouth  
- Oesophagus  
- Stomach  
- Small intestine  
- Large intestine  
- Liver  
- Pancreas  

Key idea:

- Enzymes are used to speed up digestion  

---

## 5. The Heart and Circulatory System

- The heart pumps blood around the body  

**Double circulatory system:**

- Heart → lungs → heart → body  

Blood vessels:

- **Arteries**  
  - Carry blood away from the heart  

- **Veins**  
  - Carry blood to the heart  

- **Capillaries**  
  - Very small  
  - Allow exchange of substances  

---

## 6. Blood Components

- **Red blood cells**  
  - Carry oxygen  
  - Contain haemoglobin  

- **White blood cells**  
  - Fight infection  

- **Platelets**  
  - Help blood clot  

- **Plasma**  
  - Liquid that carries everything  

---

## 7. Plant Organisation

Plants also have tissues and organs.

**Examples:**

- **Leaves**  
  - Site of photosynthesis  

- **Roots**  
  - Absorb water and minerals  

- **Stem**  
  - Supports plant and transports substances  

---

## 8. Transport in Plants

Plants transport substances using two systems:

- **Xylem**  
  - Moves water and minerals from roots to leaves  

- **Phloem**  
  - Moves sugars (food) around the plant  

Key idea:

- Transport is essential for survival just like in animals  

---

## 9. The Lungs and Gas Exchange

- Oxygen enters the blood in the lungs  
- Carbon dioxide leaves the body  

**Alveoli:**

- Tiny air sacs in the lungs  
- Large surface area  
- Thin walls  
- Good blood supply  

Key idea:

- Designed for efficient gas exchange  

---

## 10. Enzymes (Introduction)

- Enzymes are biological catalysts  
- They speed up reactions  

Key idea:

- Each enzyme has a specific shape for its substrate  

Example:

- Amylase breaks down starch  

---

- What do red blood cells carry? (Oxygen)  
- Which vessels carry blood away from the heart? (Arteries)  
- What does xylem transport? (Water and minerals)
