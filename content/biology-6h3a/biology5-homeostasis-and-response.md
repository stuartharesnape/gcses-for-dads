# GCSE Biology (Combined)  
## Chapter 5: Homeostasis and Response

---

## 1. The Big Idea (30 seconds)

- The body must keep internal conditions stable  
- This is called homeostasis  
- The nervous and hormonal systems control responses  

---

## 2. What is Homeostasis?

- Regulation of internal conditions  

Examples:

- Body temperature  
- Blood glucose levels  
- Water balance  

Key idea:

- Conditions are kept within narrow limits  

---

## 3. The Nervous System

- Detects and responds to stimuli  

Pathway:

- Stimulus → Receptor → Sensory neurone → CNS → Motor neurone → Effector  

**Effectors:**

- Muscles (contract)  
- Glands (release substances)  

---

## 4. Reflex Actions

- Rapid, automatic responses  

Example:

- Pulling hand away from a hot object  

Key idea:

- Do not involve conscious thought  

---

## 5. The Brain

Main parts:

- **Cerebrum**  
  - Conscious thought, memory, intelligence  

- **Cerebellum**  
  - Balance and coordination  

- **Medulla**  
  - Controls automatic processes (heart rate, breathing)  

---

## 6. The Eye

Structures and functions:

- **Cornea**  
  - Refracts light  

- **Lens**  
  - Focuses light onto the retina  

- **Retina**  
  - Contains light-sensitive cells  

- **Optic nerve**  
  - Carries signals to the brain  

---

## 7. Controlling Body Temperature

- Maintained around 37°C  

If too hot:

- Sweat produced  
- Blood vessels widen (vasodilation)  

If too cold:

- Shivering  
- Blood vessels narrow (vasoconstriction)  

---

## 8. The Endocrine System

- Uses hormones (chemical messengers)  
- Travel in the blood  

Slower than nervous system but longer lasting  

Examples of glands:

- Pituitary gland  
- Thyroid  
- Pancreas  

---

## 9. Blood Glucose Control

- Controlled by the pancreas  

Hormones:

- **Insulin**  
  - Lowers blood glucose  

- **Glucagon**  
  - Raises blood glucose  

Key idea:

- Keeps glucose levels stable  

---

## 10. Diabetes

- Condition where blood glucose is not controlled properly  

Types:

- **Type 1**  
  - Body does not produce insulin  

- **Type 2**  
  - Body does not respond properly to insulin  

---

## 11. Water Balance

Controlled by the kidneys.

- Remove excess water as urine  
- Controlled by hormone ADH  

Key idea:

- Maintains correct water levels  

---

## 12. Plant Hormones (Brief)

- Control growth and responses  

Example:

- **Auxin**  
  - Controls growth towards light (phototropism)  

---
