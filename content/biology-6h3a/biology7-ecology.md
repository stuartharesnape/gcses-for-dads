# GCSE Biology (Combined)  
## Chapter 7: Ecology

---

## 1. The Big Idea (30 seconds)

- Organisms interact with each other and their environment  
- Energy flows through ecosystems  
- Human activity can impact ecosystems  

---

## 2. Ecosystems

- A community of organisms and their environment  

Includes:

- Living factors (biotic)  
- Non-living factors (abiotic)  

Examples of abiotic factors:

- Temperature  
- Light intensity  
- Water availability  
- pH  

---

## 3. Interdependence

- Organisms depend on each other for survival  

Example:

- Plants provide oxygen and food  
- Animals provide carbon dioxide  

Key idea:

- Changes in one species affect others  

---

## 4. Food Chains

- Show feeding relationships  

Example:

- Grass → Rabbit → Fox  

Key idea:

- Arrows show direction of energy transfer  

---

## 5. Food Webs

- Interconnected food chains  

Key idea:

- More realistic than single food chains  

---

## 6. Trophic Levels

- Position in a food chain  

Levels:

- Producers (plants)  
- Primary consumers  
- Secondary consumers  
- Tertiary consumers  

Key idea:

- Energy decreases at each level  

---

## 7. Energy Transfer

- Not all energy is passed on  

Energy is lost as:

- Heat  
- Movement  
- Waste  

Key idea:

- Only a small percentage transfers to the next level  

---

## 8. Pyramids of Biomass

- Show the total mass of organisms at each level  

Key idea:

- Always pyramid-shaped (largest at the bottom)  

---

## 9. The Carbon Cycle

- Carbon moves between the atmosphere and living things  

Processes:

- Photosynthesis removes CO₂  
- Respiration releases CO₂  
- Decomposition releases CO₂  
- Combustion releases CO₂  

---

## 10. Decomposition

- Breakdown of dead material  

Done by:

- Microorganisms (bacteria and fungi)  

Factors affecting rate:

- Temperature  
- Oxygen  
- Water  

---

## 11. Biodiversity

- Variety of different species  

Key idea:

- High biodiversity = stable ecosystem  

---

## 12. Human Impact

Humans affect ecosystems by:

- Deforestation  
- Pollution  
- Global warming  
- Habitat destruction  

Key idea:

- Can reduce biodiversity  

---

## 13. Maintaining Biodiversity

Methods:

- Breeding programmes  
- Protecting habitats  
- Reducing pollution  
- Conservation efforts  

---

## Check Your Understanding

- What is an ecosystem? (Organisms and their environment)  
- What do arrows show in a food chain? (Energy transfer)  
- What happens to energy between trophic levels? (It is lost)  
- What is biodiversity? (Variety of species)  
- Name one human impact on ecosystems (e.g. deforestation)
