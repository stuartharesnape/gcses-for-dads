# Chemistry 10: Using Resources

## 1. The Big Idea (30 seconds)

- Earth’s resources are limited  
- We need to use materials wisely  
- Recycling and sustainability are key ideas  
- Chemists help reduce environmental impact  

---

## 2. Finite and Renewable Resources

Finite resources:

- Will run out  
- Form over millions of years  

Examples:

- Fossil fuels  
- Metals  

Renewable resources:

- Replaced naturally  

Examples:

- Wind  
- Solar energy  
- Timber  

Key idea:

- Sustainable use is important  

---

## 3. Potable Water

Potable water is safe to drink.

Requirements:

- Low levels of dissolved salts  
- Free from harmful microbes  

How it is produced:

- Filtration  
- Sterilisation  

In some places:

- Desalination is used  

Key idea:

- Access to clean water is essential  

---

## 4. Waste Water Treatment

Waste water must be treated before release.

Stages:

- Screening (removes large objects)  
- Sedimentation (solids settle)  
- Biological processes (bacteria break down waste)  

Key idea:

- Prevents pollution  

---

## 5. Life Cycle Assessment (LCA)

LCA looks at environmental impact.

Stages:

- Extracting raw materials  
- Manufacturing  
- Use  
- Disposal  

Key idea:

- Helps compare products  

---

## 6. Recycling

Recycling reduces:

- Waste sent to landfill  
- Use of raw materials  
- Energy consumption  

Examples:

- Metals  
- Glass  
- Plastics  

Key idea:

- Saves energy compared to making new materials  

---

## 7. Corrosion and Protection

Corrosion is the destruction of metals.

Example:

- Iron rusting  

Conditions needed:

- Oxygen  
- Water  

Prevention methods:

- Painting  
- Coating with oil or grease  
- Galvanising (coating with zinc)  

---

## 8. Alloys

Alloys are mixtures of metals.

- Designed to improve properties  

Examples:

- Steel (iron + carbon)  
- Brass (copper + zinc)  

Key idea:

- Alloys are stronger than pure metals  

---

## 9. Fertilisers (Brief Overview)

Plants need nutrients:

- Nitrogen  
- Phosphorus  
- Potassium  

Fertilisers supply these.

Key idea:

- Improves crop yield  

---

## 10. Check Your Understanding

- What is a finite resource? ( will run out )  
- What is potable water? ( safe to drink )  
- What does LCA measure? ( environmental impact )  
- Why recycle? ( reduce waste and energy use )
