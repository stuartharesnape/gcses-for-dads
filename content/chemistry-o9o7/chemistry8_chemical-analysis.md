# Chemistry 8: Chemical Analysis

## 1. The Big Idea (30 seconds)

- Chemists need to identify what substances are present  
- Pure substances behave in predictable ways  
- Mixtures can be separated and analysed  
- There are simple tests to identify common gases  

---

## 2. Pure Substances

A pure substance is:

- A single element or compound  
- Not mixed with anything else  

Key idea:

- Pure substances have fixed melting and boiling points  

---

## 3. Formulations

Formulations are useful mixtures.

- Designed to have specific properties  

Examples:

- Fuels  
- Medicines  
- Paints  

Key idea:

- Exact amounts are carefully controlled  

---

## 4. Chromatography

Used to separate mixtures.

- Often used for inks or dyes  

Process:

- A solvent moves through paper  
- Different substances move at different speeds  

Key idea:

- Each substance forms a spot  

---

## 5. Rf Value (Higher Tier concept, but useful)

Rf = distance moved by substance ÷ distance moved by solvent  

Key idea:

- Helps identify substances  

---

## 6. Identifying Gases

Common gas tests:

Hydrogen:

- Burns with a squeaky pop  

Oxygen:

- Relights a glowing splint  

Carbon dioxide:

- Turns limewater cloudy  

Chlorine:

- Bleaches damp litmus paper  

---

## 7. Testing for Ions (Brief Overview)

Flame tests:

- Different metals produce different colours  

Examples:

- Sodium → yellow  
- Potassium → lilac  
- Copper → green  

Key idea:

- Used to identify metal ions  

---

## 8. Required Practical (Typical GCSE)

Students may:

- Use chromatography to separate inks  
- Measure distances and calculate Rf  

Key idea:

- Careful measurement is important  

---

## 9. Check Your Understanding

- What is a pure substance? ( single element or compound )  
- What is chromatography used for? ( separating mixtures )  
- What does Rf measure? ( movement of substance )  
- What test identifies oxygen? ( glowing splint relights )  
- What colour is sodium in a flame test? ( yellow )
