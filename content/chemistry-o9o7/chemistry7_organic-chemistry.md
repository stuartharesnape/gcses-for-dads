# Chemistry 7: Organic Chemistry

## 1. The Big Idea (30 seconds)

- Organic chemistry is the chemistry of carbon compounds  
- Hydrocarbons are fuels made of hydrogen and carbon  
- There are two main types: alkanes and alkenes  
- These fuels can be burned or broken down into useful products  

---

## 2. Hydrocarbons

Hydrocarbons contain only hydrogen and carbon.

- Found in crude oil and natural gas  

Examples:

- Methane  
- Ethane  
- Propane  

Key idea:

- They are fuels  

---

## 3. Alkanes

Alkanes are saturated hydrocarbons.

- Only single bonds between carbon atoms  

General formula:

- CₙH₂ₙ₊₂  

Examples:

- Methane (CH₄)  
- Ethane (C₂H₆)  

Properties:

- Relatively unreactive  
- Burn easily  

---

## 4. Combustion

Burning hydrocarbons in oxygen.

Complete combustion:

- Produces carbon dioxide and water  

Example:

- Methane + oxygen → carbon dioxide + water  

Incomplete combustion (limited oxygen):

- Produces carbon monoxide or carbon (soot)  

Key idea:

- Carbon monoxide is toxic  

---

## 5. Alkenes

Alkenes are unsaturated hydrocarbons.

- Contain at least one double bond  

General formula:

- CₙH₂ₙ  

Examples:

- Ethene (C₂H₄)  

Properties:

- More reactive than alkanes  

Test for alkenes:

- Bromine water turns from orange to colourless  

---

## 6. Cracking

Cracking breaks long hydrocarbons into smaller ones.

- Done using heat and a catalyst  

Produces:

- Alkanes and alkenes  

Why it is useful:

- Produces more useful fuels  
- Produces alkenes for making plastics  

---

## 7. Polymers (Brief Link)

Alkenes can form polymers.

- Many small molecules join to form long chains  

Example:

- Ethene → poly(ethene)  

Key idea:

- Used to make plastics  

---

## 8. Problems with Fuels

Burning fuels causes issues:

- Carbon dioxide → climate change  
- Carbon monoxide → toxic  
- Sulfur dioxide → acid rain  
- Particulates → health problems  

---

- What is different about alkenes? ( double bond )
